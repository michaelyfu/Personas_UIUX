Analysis of Sci Li Phone Charging Booth

Overview

The Sci Li phone charging station is located in the basement of the Sci Li and allows students to charge their phones while studying. The charging station is similar to a locker rental. There are 8 phone charging units that students can use for any period of time. These stations are kept secure by users setting their own passcodes for their units.


Problem/Solution: Many students require their phone to be charged and/or remove distractions.


The Sci Li phone charging station solves these problems by 1) providing students with a public, secure phone charger and 2) removing their phones from potentially distracting them during study sessions.

    Diagram

  1. Click this button to begin your interaction with the charging station. The button brings the page to a new screen that asks the user to pick which phone unit he/she wants to open.
  2. Click this button to retrieve your phone. The button brings the user to a page where he/she picks the storage unit the phone is in, and then asks for what the user-set password is.
  3. This is the help button. There is nothing currently on display after clicking the help button.
  4. The edge around the phone unit indicates to the user to push down to close. Users push this area when putting their phone in to charge.
  5. The red/blue backlight helps indicate to the user whether a particular phone unit is in use or not.

Interviews

Interview Questions

1. How did you learn about the charging booth and when did you start using it?
2. Why do you use the phone charger?
3. Do you use the charging booth frequently when you come to the library? Why or why not?
4. What are the pros and cons of using this phone charger?
5. Do you find the phone charger difficult or easy to use? Why or why not?
6. How has your overall experience been with these charging booths in the Sci li?

Interview 1—Lucia

  • Learned about charging booth from a friend last week
  • Friend stated it helped her focus
  • Uses booth when studying late at night and phone battery is low
  • Intuitive interface because of the buttons and labels
  • Overall postive experience, but wishes he could use phone while charging

Interview 2—Siming

  • First learned about charging booth freshman year after seeing it from a distance
  • Did not use booth until later because she didn't know booth would charge Androids
  • Uses booth primarily to charge phone but also remove distractions
  • Finds interface easy to use
  • Small design choices, such as the grayed out buttons, make interface more clear

Interview 3—Laura

  • Uses booth to charge phone and remove phone distraction
  • A bit worried that someone could guess her password and take her phone
  • Wishes she didn't have to miss calls and text messages
  • Would enjoy charging experience more if she could rent out portable chargers

Key Observations

1. Students who use the booth typically come from entering the basement.
2. Most students interact with the touchscreen very quickly.
3. Students who charge their phone keave to sit down in the basement.
4. After retrieving their phones, students will usually leave the Sci Li.
5. The help button contains no information.

Interview Summary

1. Students varied in how frequently they used the charger.
2. Students did not have complaints around the UI.
3. Students found the UI easy to use.
4. Students used the booth for both charging phone and staying focused.
5. Students enjoyed the small design choices, such as grayed out buttons.

Personas

Unprepared Ulysses is a 1st year Computer Science student at Brown University that is addicted to video games. A self-proclaimed "God of Gaming," he spends most of his day playing Valorant with his friends on Discord. With a midterm coming up for his Data Structures course and no lectures attended, Ulysses is trying to cram the night before the exam.

Ulysses represents a CS student at Brown that has to cram for a midterm the next day since he hasn't been going to lecture and reviewing the material. However, Ulysses is easily distracted and wants to seek a distraction-free zone. He typically does homework in his room, but inevitably results in him aimlessly scrolling through TikTok or playing Valorant. Ulysses tries going to the Sci Li after hearing about the charging booth from a friend to remove his phone as a distraction. Many students like Ulysses will only use the interface when they want to fully focus on their work.

Consistent Connie is another 1st year Computer Science student at Brown University that packs everything onto her Notion, inputs assignments into her Google Calendar on the first day of classes, and holds leadership positions in 3 different clubs on campus (as a freshman).


Connie represents a CS student at Brown that one may describe as the archetypal "good student." She wakes up at 7 am every morning for her yoga class, and hits the Sci Li after classes end ~3 pm each day. Since she is so busy and starts her mornings so early, she uses Notion and Google Calendar on her phone to help keep track of her day. However, she notices that her phone always seems to dip below 20% battery charge in the later afternoons. So, she uses the Sci Li phone charger booth to charge her phone, but also remove distractions so she can focus on school assignments. When first coming to charge her phone, Connie clicked the "help" button to figure out how to use the machine. However, no information popped up, and she thought the machine was broken. She only began using the device after seeing someone else use the machine.

Storyboard

Ulysses is a freshman at Brown University studying computer science. On the first day of classes, Ulysses is already falling asleep in lectures.

4 Weeks Pass. It turns out that Ulysses has been spending his time gaming with his friends instead of going to classes.

Ulysses wakes up at 2 pm after a long night of gaming to a notification on his phone. Oh no!

Ulysses has a midterm tomorrow! Ulysses decides to go to class to get a topic of the exam topics covered.

While sitting in class, he realizes he has no notes from the past month and begins to panic a little. He decides that he needs to start studying ASAP.

After class, Ulysses rushes to the Scili.

Oh no! Ulysses has spent the spent the past 3 hours scrolling through TikTok! He notices his phone is also at 11%. In an effort to remove his phone as a distraction and to charge his phone, Ulysses walks over to the Charging Station.

Ulysses learned about the charging station from a friend, and very rarely uses it. However, the UI is still simple for him to use. At the charging station, Ulysses first clicks the Charge button.

Ulysses then selects that he wannts to use locker 3.

Ulysses sets a secure password—0101.

Ulysses plugs his phone in, thinking his phone is now going to be charged.

When Ulysses comes back to use the charger, he clicks the Retrieve button.

Ulysses now inputs his previously set password—0101.

The locker opens and he pulls out his phone.

He is glad that he was able to study for the exam without distractions and has a fuly charged phone!
